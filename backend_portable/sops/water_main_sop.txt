MUNICIPAL ENGINEERING STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Water Distribution and Sewerage — Emergency Response
Water Main Break and Pipe Burst Response Plan
Document: MCD-SOP-WATER-003 | Version 1.8 | January 2024
Issuing Authority: Delhi Jal Board / Urban Local Body Engineering Division

================================================================
SECTION 1 — SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY
================================================================
This SOP covers emergency response procedures for:
- Water main breaks (distribution mains, trunk mains)
- Service line bursts
- Sewer line collapses and overflows
- Hydrant failures and road flooding from water infrastructure

Applicable Standards:
- CPHEEO Manual on Water Supply and Treatment (Third Edition)
- IS:12288 — Code of Practice for Water Supply
- IS:4127 — Laying and Jointing of Stoneware Pipes
- National Disaster Management Guidelines (Urban Flooding)

================================================================
SECTION 2 — IMMEDIATE RESPONSE (FIRST 2 HOURS)
================================================================
2.1 Alert and Notification Protocol
Upon receiving water main break report:
- JE/AE on duty notified within 15 minutes
- Isolation valve location confirmed from GIS/asset map within 30 minutes
- Traffic police informed for road closure coordination
- Utility corridor notification: electricity, gas, telecom (call 1912 / IGN)

2.2 Isolation Procedure
Step 1: Identify isolation valve(s) from pipe network schematic
Step 2: Two-person team at each valve — never operate alone
Step 3: Close valves slowly (max. 1 revolution per 5 seconds) to prevent water hammer
Step 4: Confirm isolation — flow at break location should reduce significantly
Step 5: Note all consumers/areas affected by isolation
Step 6: Activate alternative supply (tanker deployment) for affected consumers

2.3 Immediate Safety Measures
- Establish 15m exclusion zone around break point (increase to 30m if flooding)
- Install pumps to remove standing water from carriageway within 30 minutes
- Check for undermining of road structure — probe 1m around break
- Isolate electricity supply to any surface/basement services in flood zone
- Contact gas utility if gas mains are in proximity of break

2.4 Evacuation Criteria
Forced evacuation required if:
- Water pressure exceeds 2.5 bar at break (main >300mm diameter)
- Pavement collapse deeper than 500mm
- Flooding encroaches on residential ground floors
- Gas leak detected in conjunction with break

================================================================
SECTION 3 — SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================
3.1 PPE — Mandatory for all water main repair crew
- Waterproof overalls/waders for workers in flooded areas
- High-visibility vest (reflective)
- Hard hat with chin strap (in confined space operations)
- Safety boots (waterproof, steel-toe)
- Nitrile gloves (water contact) + leather work gloves (excavation)
- Eye protection during pipe cutting/joining
- Confined space equipment if working below 1.2m depth (respiratory protection, harness, tripod)

3.2 Confined Space Entry (excavations > 1.2m)
Mandatory requirements per IS:4912:
- Atmospheric testing (oxygen, combustible gas, H2S) before entry
- Mechanical ventilation — continuous forced fresh air
- Rescue/standby team at surface level at all times
- Communication maintained between entrant and standby
- Emergency extraction plan in place

3.3 Traffic Management
Water main repairs typically require full road closure on carriageway.
Setup per IRC:67 and Street Works:
- Temporary traffic signals or one-lane working
- Advance warning 100m minimum
- Diversionary route signed and lit
- Night-time working: additional lighting (2 lux minimum on carriageway)

================================================================
SECTION 4 — EXCAVATION AND PIPE ACCESS
================================================================
4.1 Excavation Safety
Maximum unsupported excavation: 1.2m deep (IS:3764)
Shoring required: all excavations > 1.2m OR in loose/made ground
Options: hydraulic shoring, sheet piling, trench boxes
Slope excavation: 45° maximum in stable ground, 30° in soft soil

4.2 Pipe Exposure Protocol
Step 1: Hand dig 500mm around pipe location before mechanical excavation
Step 2: Expose pipe for minimum 600mm on each side of break
Step 3: Pump all water from excavation — zero standing water during repair
Step 4: Clean pipe surface thoroughly — remove all soil, corrosion, debris
Step 5: Mark pipe material and diameter on work order form

4.3 Pipe Identification
Common pipe materials in Indian municipal networks:
- Cast Iron (CI): Pre-1980 installations, grey colour, heavy
- Ductile Iron (DI): Post-1985, black epoxy coated
- Asbestos Cement (AC): 1960–1990, treat as hazardous material
- HDPE/PVC: Modern installations, plastic
Note: AC pipe must be handled per Asbestos Safety Guidelines — wet before handling,
double-bag for disposal, do not cut without respiratory protection.

================================================================
SECTION 5 — PIPE REPAIR PROCEDURES
================================================================
5.1 Temporary Repair (for immediate service restoration)
Materials: Pipe repair clamp (saddle clamp), stainless steel bands
Procedure:
Step 1: Select clamp sized to pipe OD ± 5mm
Step 2: Position clamp centrally over break/crack
Step 3: Tighten bands evenly to 50 Nm torque
Step 4: Slowly restore water supply — test at 1.1× working pressure
Step 5: Monitor for 30 minutes before leaving site
Expected life: 6–12 months (must be followed by permanent pipe replacement)

5.2 Pipe Section Replacement (Permanent Repair)
5.2.1 CI/DI Pipe (flanged or mechanical couplings)
Step 1: Cut out damaged section using angle grinder or pipe cutter
Step 2: Cut replacement section to exact length (allow 5mm tolerance)
Step 3: Clean pipe ends and coupling seating faces
Step 4: Install rubber gaskets in correct position
Step 5: Assemble coupling/flanged joint — tighten bolts in diagonal pattern
Step 6: Torque bolts to manufacturer specification (typically 40–80 Nm for DN100–300)

5.2.2 HDPE Pipe (butt fusion or electrofusion)
Step 1: Cut pipe with pipe cutter — ends must be square (< 0.5° out of square)
Step 2: Scrape oxide layer from pipe ends (minimum 25mm from end)
Step 3: Butt fusion: heat plate 200°C ± 10°C, join per ISO 21307
Step 4: Allow cooling period — minimum 30 minutes before pressurising
Step 5: Visual inspection of bead profile (uniform, no voids)

5.3 Pressure Testing (Mandatory before backfill)
Hydrostatic test per IS:2065:
- Test pressure: 1.5× maximum working pressure for 1 hour
- Allowable leakage: zero (zero tolerance for potable water mains)
- Record gauge readings every 15 minutes
- STOP test immediately if gauge drops — locate additional leak

================================================================
SECTION 6 — BACKFILL AND REINSTATEMENT
================================================================
6.1 Backfill Material and Compaction
Layer 1 (pipe bedding, 150mm each side): Selected granular material, pea gravel
Layer 2 (surround, up to 300mm above pipe): Selected soil, no stones > 25mm
Compact layer 2 in 150mm lifts to 95% Proctor
Layer 3 (sub-base): Granular sub-base per MoRTH Table 400-1, compact to 98%
Layer 4 (surface reinstatement): Per Road Repair SOP MCD-SOP-ROAD-001

6.2 Surface Reinstatement
Temporary reinstatement: Cold mix asphalt (open to traffic within 4 hours)
Permanent reinstatement: Hot mix within 28 days per Street Works Code

================================================================
SECTION 7 — CREW COMPOSITION
================================================================
Emergency water main repair crew (minimum):
- 1 × Assistant Engineer (AE) — Site Supervisor
- 1 × Foreman — direct crew supervision
- 2 × Plumbers (licensed)
- 4 × Labourers (excavation, backfill)
- 1 × Machine operator (if mini-excavator used)
- 1 × Traffic marshal

================================================================
SECTION 8 — EQUIPMENT
================================================================
Standard emergency response equipment:
- Mini-excavator (1.5–3 tonne class)
- Submersible dewatering pump (80–100mm outlet, minimum 500 L/min)
- Pipe cutter / angle grinder
- Hydraulic trench shoring or trench box
- Pipe repair clamps (various sizes: DN100, DN150, DN200, DN250, DN300)
- Welding/fusion equipment (for HDPE repairs)
- Hydrostatic test pump and pressure gauge
- Traffic management signs and barriers
- Generator and lighting for night work
- Emergency water tankers (coordinate with supply control room)
