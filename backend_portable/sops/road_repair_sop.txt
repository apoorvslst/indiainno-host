MUNICIPAL ENGINEERING STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Road Surface Maintenance and Pothole Repair
Document: MCD-SOP-ROAD-001 | Version 2.4 | April 2024
Issuing Authority: Public Works Department, Engineering Division

================================================================
SECTION 1 — SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY
================================================================
This SOP applies to all road surface defects including potholes, alligator cracking,
rutting, edge failures, and surface depressions on municipal roads classified as:
- Primary Arterial Roads (carriageway width > 7.5m)
- Secondary Arterial Roads (carriageway width 5.5m – 7.5m)
- Local Roads and Lanes (carriageway width < 5.5m)

Equivalent Indian Standards: IRC:SP:16 (Surface Evenness of Highway Pavements),
MoRTH Specifications for Road and Bridge Works (5th Revision), IRC:37-2018.

================================================================
SECTION 2 — DEFECT CLASSIFICATION AND SEVERITY
================================================================
2.1 Severity Levels
LOW SEVERITY: Pothole diameter < 15cm OR depth < 3cm. No immediate safety risk.
Repair timeline: Within 72 hours of reporting.

MEDIUM SEVERITY: Pothole diameter 15–40cm OR depth 3–10cm. Vehicle damage risk.
Repair timeline: Within 48 hours of reporting.

HIGH SEVERITY: Pothole diameter > 40cm OR depth > 10cm OR on high-traffic arterial route.
Repair timeline: Within 24 hours of reporting. Traffic management mandatory.

CRITICAL SEVERITY: Structural failure, sinkhole formation, or pavement collapse.
Repair timeline: Immediate response within 6 hours. Emergency declaration required.
Escalate to Superintending Engineer immediately.

2.2 Defect Measurement Protocol
All defects must be measured using:
- Steel tape for diameter/length/width
- Depth gauge or ruler for depth measurement
- Photographic evidence (min. 3 photos: aerial, side, scaled reference)
Document in Form PWD-DR-07 before repair work begins.

================================================================
SECTION 3 — PRE-REPAIR SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================
3.1 Traffic Management
All repair sites require Traffic Management Plan (TMP) per IRC:67-2012.
Minimum setup:
- Advance warning signs: 50m from site (urban), 100m (highway)
- Road closed/lane closed signs at site entry
- Cones/delineators at 5m intervals for site boundary
- At least one traffic marshal for sites on roads with AADT > 5000 vpd

3.2 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) — Mandatory for all crew
- High-visibility vest (Class 2 minimum, EN ISO 20471)
- Hard hat (IS:2925 compliant)
- Safety boots with steel toecap and midsole (IS:15298)
- Work gloves for material handling
- Eye protection when using cutting equipment or compactors

3.3 Hazard Assessment
Before commencing work, Site Engineer must assess:
a) Underground utilities: contact utility departments for clearance (min. 48h notice)
b) Traffic flow and sight distance
c) Overhead obstructions
d) Proximity to drainage — prevent asphalt from entering storm drains
e) Weather: Do NOT apply bituminous material if rain expected within 12 hours
   or surface temperature below 10°C or above 45°C

================================================================
SECTION 4 — REPAIR MATERIALS AND SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================
4.1 Cold Mix Asphalt (Temporary Repair)
Use when: emergency/interim repair, weather prohibits hot mix, remote location.
Specification: Emulsion-based cold mix per IRC:SP:100-2014
Application temperature: Ambient (5°C – 45°C)
Compaction: Minimum 10 passes with plate compactor (300kg min.)
Expected life: 3–6 months. Must be followed by permanent repair.
Cost estimate: INR 8,000 – 12,000 per tonne (2024 rates)

4.2 Dense Bituminous Macadam (DBM) — Permanent Base Course
Layer thickness: 50mm minimum
Bitumen grade: VG-30 for plains, VG-40 for high-altitude/temperature zones
Mix temperature: 155°C – 170°C (at plant)
Laying temperature: Minimum 130°C
Compaction: Steel roller (8-10 tonne) to achieve minimum 92% Marshall density
Cost estimate: INR 6,500 – 9,500 per tonne (2024 rates)

4.3 Bituminous Concrete (BC) — Surface Course
Layer thickness: 25–40mm
Bitumen grade: VG-30
Mix design: Marshall method per MoRTH Section 500
Laying temperature: Minimum 125°C
Compaction sequence: Breakdown roller (10t) → Intermediate (8t) → Finish (8t)
Minimum passes: 8 breakdown, 4 intermediate, 2 finish
Cost estimate: INR 9,000 – 14,000 per tonne (2024 rates)

4.4 Tack Coat
Apply before each bituminous layer. CSS-1 emulsion @ 0.25–0.30 kg/m²
Allow tack coat to break fully before laying mix.

================================================================
SECTION 5 — STANDARD REPAIR PROCEDURE (POTHOLE)
================================================================
5.1 Site Assessment (Duration: 1–2 hours)
Step 1: Erect traffic management per Section 3.1
Step 2: Measure and document defect per Section 2.2
Step 3: Assess depth — determine if sub-base failure is present
Step 4: Mark repair area: rectangular/square cuts at least 30cm beyond defect boundary
Step 5: Photograph completed assessment

5.2 Excavation and Preparation (Duration: 2–4 hours)
Step 1: Cut edges vertically using road saw (or cold milling machine for large areas)
        Cut lines must be straight and perpendicular to road axis
Step 2: Remove all loose/failed material using jackhammer, pneumatic drill, or excavator
Step 3: Remove spoil to approved disposal site (not road verge)
Step 4: Compact subgrade to 95% Proctor density (MoRTH Clause 305.3.3)
Step 5: If base failure detected: add granular sub-base (GSB) layer 150mm, compact
Step 6: Apply prime coat (MC-30) @ 0.6–1.0 kg/m² on granular base

5.3 Base Course Repairs (Duration: 2–6 hours)
Step 1: Lay DBM in maximum 75mm uncompacted lifts
Step 2: Compact immediately — do not allow temperature to drop below 130°C
Step 3: Check for voids and irregularities; add material as needed
Step 4: Apply tack coat before surface course

5.4 Surface Course Application (Duration: 2–4 hours)
Step 1: Apply bituminous concrete surface course (Section 4.3)
Step 2: Level to match existing road profile ± 3mm (check with 3m straight edge)
Step 3: Compact per sequence in Section 4.3
Step 4: Allow to cool to ambient temperature before opening to traffic (minimum 2 hours)

5.5 Quality Inspection (Duration: 1–2 hours)
Step 1: Visual inspection for surface texture, aggregate distribution
Step 2: Ride quality check with 3m straight edge — maximum unevenness: 5mm
Step 3: Core sample (one per 500 m² of repair area) for density testing
Step 4: Complete Form PWD-QC-12 (Quality Control Record)
Step 5: Obtain sign-off from Site Engineer and Quality Inspector

5.6 Site Reinstatement
Remove all traffic management equipment
Clean road surface of all debris, bitumen spills
Remove temporary signage
Photograph completed repair (minimum 3 photos)

================================================================
SECTION 6 — EQUIPMENT LIST
================================================================
Standard equipment for pothole repair (medium severity):
- Road saw or angle grinder with diamond blade
- Pneumatic drill (jackhammer) or small excavator
- Air compressor
- Bituminous mix transport vehicle (insulated body)
- Plate compactor (300–600 kg) for small patches
- Tandem roller (8–10 tonne) for large patches
- Traffic management equipment (cones, signs, barriers)
- Tack coat sprayer or watering can
- Straight edge (3m)
- Depth gauge

================================================================
SECTION 7 — CREW COMPOSITION
================================================================
Minimum crew for medium-severity pothole repair:
- 1 × Site Engineer (Class I PWD)
- 1 × Foreman
- 4–6 × Unskilled workers (operators, helpers)
- 1 × Quality Inspector (for permanent repair)
- Traffic marshals as per TMP

================================================================
SECTION 8 — COST ESTIMATION GUIDELINES
================================================================
Approximate cost ranges (2024, Delhi NCR):
Small patch (<1 m²): INR 8,000 – 15,000
Medium patch (1–5 m²): INR 15,000 – 55,000
Large repair (5–20 m²): INR 55,000 – 2,50,000
Full road resurfacing (per lane-km): INR 25,00,000 – 45,00,000

Include in cost estimate:
- Materials (asphalt, tack coat, GSB)
- Labour (daily wages per Schedule of Rates)
- Equipment hire (road saw, roller, compactor)
- Traffic management hire
- Disposal of excavated material
- Contingency: 10% of direct cost

================================================================
SECTION 9 — REPORTING AND RECORDS
================================================================
All work orders must reference the original complaint ticket number.
Forms required:
- Form PWD-DR-07: Defect Record (before repair)
- Form PWD-MT-03: Material Receipt and Usage
- Form PWD-QC-12: Quality Control Record (after repair)
- Form PWD-WC-01: Work Completion Certificate

Records retained for minimum 7 years per RTI Act requirements.
